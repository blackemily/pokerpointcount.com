Most Players Bluff Too Much Or Not Enough

Bluffing is one of the most misunderstood skills in poker.

Some players are afraid to bluff. They wait for strong hands and miss profitable opportunities.

Others bluff constantly. They mistake activity for aggression and aggression for profit.

A good bluff creates pressure. A bad bluff creates losses.

The difference is understanding when a bluff is actually a plus-one decision.

Stop Bluffing Because You Feel Like It

Many bluffs happen for emotional reasons.

  • Frustration
  • Boredom
  • Ego
  • Revenge
  • Impatience

These reasons may feel powerful in the moment, but they are often expensive.

Bluffing Systems teaches you how to separate emotional aggression from profitable aggression.

The Checkpoint Approach To Bluffing

Bluffing should never be automatic.

Every bluff creates risk. Every bluff should have a purpose.

Before committing chips, the Checkpoint Poker system asks a simple question:

Is this bluff creating a genuine plus-one opportunity, or is it creating unnecessary risk?

The strongest bluffers are not reckless. They are selective.

They understand ranges, pressure, board texture, opponent tendencies, and timing.

More importantly, they understand when not to bluff.
