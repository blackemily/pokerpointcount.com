Strong Players Think In Ranges

Weak players ask, “What hand does he have?”

Stronger players ask, “What range of hands can he have?”

That difference changes everything. Poker becomes clearer when you stop trying to guess one exact holding and start understanding the full group of hands that make sense.

Range construction turns poker from guessing into structured decision-making.

Ranges Are Not Just Charts

Many players think range construction means memorizing preflop charts.

Charts can help, but they are not the whole skill.

Real range construction means understanding how ranges are built, narrowed, protected, attacked, and adjusted through every street of a hand.

  • What hands should continue against pressure?
  • What hands belong in a value range?
  • What hands belong in a bluffing range?
  • What hands should be removed after each action?
  • How does stack depth change the range?

The Checkpoint Approach To Ranges

Range construction is part of the Checkpoint Poker decision system.

Every action creates a checkpoint. Every checkpoint affects the range.

When you bet, call, raise, check, or fold, your range changes. When your opponent acts, their range changes too.

The goal is to understand how those ranges move so you can make better decisions with incomplete information.

Better ranges create better decisions. Better decisions create better long-term results.
